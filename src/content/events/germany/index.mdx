faq:
  - q: Are spots limited? Can I register later?
    a: Yes, spots are limited to preserve the experience on the road. Registration
      opens to the waitlist first, then to the public — it's almost always sold
      out before opening to all.
  - q: How do I register?
    a: Create an account on bikeadventureseries.com and join the Germany waitlist.
      When registration opens you'll receive an email with early access.
  - q: Do I need a medical certificate?
    a: No medical certificate is required. This is not a race — just bring common
      sense, a bike in good condition and your own sense of pace.
  - q: Are all the routes on roads?
    a: Yes, the entire route is on tarmac. We hand-pick backroads, cycle paths and
      quiet connections so you focus on the landscape, not on traffic.
  - q: What bike should I bring?
    a: A road or endurance bike with 28–32 mm tires works perfectly. Gravel bikes
      are welcome too. No special tire choice needed — it's all asphalt.
  - q: Are there time limits or checkpoints?
    a: None. No cutoffs, no mandatory checkpoints, no leaderboard. You ride the 500
      km over four days at your own pace.
  - q: Where do I sleep and eat?
    a: You choose — hotels, B&Bs, alpine huts along the route. We share suggestions
      in the digital guide but the daily logistics stay in your hands, so the
      trip feels like yours.
  - q: What if I register and can't participate?
    a: You can transfer your spot to another rider until a few weeks before the
      event. Full cancellation policy is in the rider dashboard after
      registration.
  - q: Start and finish are the same place?
    a: Yes — it's a loop starting and finishing in Innsbruck. Easier logistics, one
      base, no shuttle needed.
  - q: What about parking and getting there?
    a: Innsbruck has a central train station reachable from most of Europe. If you
      drive, long-stay parking options are shared in the event dashboard.
