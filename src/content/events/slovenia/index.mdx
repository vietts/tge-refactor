    a: "Yes, the entire route is on tarmac. We hand-pick backroads, cycle paths

    a: "A road or endurance bike with 28–32 mm tires works perfectly. Gravel

    a: "None. No cutoffs, no mandatory checkpoints, no leaderboard. You ride the
      550 km at your own pace, over the days that suit you."
  - q: "Where do I sleep and eat?"
    a: "You choose — hotels, B&Bs, alpine huts along the route. We share
      suggestions in the digital guide but the daily logistics stay in your
      hands, so the trip feels like yours."
  - q: "What if I register and can't participate?"
    a: "You can transfer your spot to another rider until a few weeks before the
      event. Full cancellation policy is in the rider dashboard after
      registration."
  - q: "Are the start and finish in the same place?"
    a: "Yes — it's a loop starting and finishing in Udine. Easier logistics, one
      base, no shuttle needed."
  - q: "How do I reach Udine?"
    a: "Udine is easy to reach from across Europe. Closest airports are Trieste
      (40km, direct train in 32–40 min, bikes welcome), Venice Marco Polo (~2h
      via ATVO bus + train from Mestre), and Treviso (~90 min by bus + train).
      By train: Udine is on the Vienna–Rome line; Trenitalia regional trains
      accept bikes with a small supplement. By car: A23/A4 intersection with
      plenty of long-stay parking."
  - q: "Where can I leave my bike box?"
    a: "Secure bike-box storage is available near the start/finish in Udine —
      details and addresses are shared in the event dashboard after registration."
  - q: "I have never done bikepacking — what do I need?"
    a: "A bike in good working order, two small bags (handlebar + saddle or
      frame), basic repair tools, and the will to enjoy the road. The digital
      guide walks you through bike setup, packing, and pacing — you don't need
      racing experience, just a bit of curiosity."

From Udine you follow the Tagliamento — Europe's last wild river — then cross into Slovenia past Austro-Hungarian fortifications. Emerald rivers, alpine passes, enchanted forests, and roads so quiet you'll forget traffic exists. 550 km, 100% on tarmac, mostly on dedicated cycle paths: your bike is perfect for this.
