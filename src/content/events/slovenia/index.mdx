faq:
  - q: This edition is sold out. Can I still join?
    a: No, sorry. Leave your email to join the 2027 waitlist. You'll get priority
      access to the next edition, plus the route preview and packing list in the
      meantime.
  - q: How do I register when 2027 opens?
    a: Create an account on bikeadventureseries.com and join the Italy/Slovenia
      waitlist. When registration opens, we email the list first — it almost
      always sells out before opening publicly.
  - q: Do I need a medical certificate?
    a: Yes, it is required for your insurance (not optional). You don't need to
      upload it during registration; you can get it later. We'll provide a
      template for non-Italian residents. For Italian residents, you'll need a
      "Certificato Medico Agonistico specifico per il ciclismo"
  - q: Are all routes on roads?
    a: Yes, the entire route is on tarmac. We hand-pick backroads, cycle paths and
      quiet connections so you focus on the landscape, not on traffic — about
      60% cycle paths, 40% quiet secondary roads.
  - q: What bike should I bring?
    a: Gravel, road or endurance bike with 28–32 mm tires works perfectly. E-Bikes
      are welcome too.
  - q: Any time limits or checkpoints?
    a: None. No cutoffs, no mandatory checkpoints, no leaderboard. You ride the 550
      km at your own pace, over the days that suit you.
  - q: Where do I sleep and eat?
    a: There are plenty of hotels and B&Bs along the route. We share suggestions in
      the digital guide, but the daily logistics stay in your hands, so you can
      plan your stages as you wish.
  - q: What if I register and can't participate?
    a: As per the rules, which you must read before registering, there are no
      refunds, partial refunds, or transfers of registration to the following
      year or other editions.
  - q: Are the start and finish in the same place?
    a: Yes, it's a loop that starts and finishes in Udine. Easier logistics!
  - q: How do I reach Udine?
    a: "Udine is easy to reach from across Europe. Closest airports are Trieste
      (40km, direct train in 32–40 min, bikes welcome), Venice Marco Polo (~2h
      via ATVO bus + train from Mestre), and Treviso (~90 min by bus + train).
      By train: Udine is on the Vienna–Rome line; Trenitalia regional trains
      accept bikes with a small supplement. By car: A23/A4 intersection with
      plenty of long-stay parking."
  - q: Where can I leave my bike box?
    a: Secure bike-box storage is available near the start/finish in Udine — details
      and addresses are shared in the event dashboard after registration.
  - q: I have never done bikepacking — what do I need?
    a: A bike in good working order, two small bags (handlebar + saddle or frame),
      basic repair tools, and the will to enjoy the road. The digital guide
      walks you through bike setup, packing, and pacing. You don't need
      experience, just a bit of curiosity.

From Udine you follow the Tagliamento — Europe's last wild river — then cross into Slovenia past Austro-Hungarian fortifications. Emerald rivers, alpine passes, enchanted forests, and roads so quiet you'll forget traffic exists. 550 km, 100% on tarmac, mostly on dedicated cycle paths: your bike is perfect for this.
